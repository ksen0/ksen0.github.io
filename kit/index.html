Kit Kuksenok, PhD

I've been doing some form of code work since 2005 (when I taught, as a teaching assistant, the intro to programming in Java at Carnegie Mellon's Andrew's Leap program for high school students).

For my Master's, I worked in the intersection of machine translation and human-computer interaction; for my PhD, I studied software production practices. Here is where you can find my research CV or scientific publications.

Since February 2018, I am senior R&D engineer at jobpal Ltd, where I mostly work with natural-language short-form text, mostly in English and German.

KSEN ZERO (k0)

I was born in Kiev, Ukraine. I did most of my growing up in the USA (Pittsburgh, Oberlin, Seattle). Since 2016, I have been based in Berlin. I speak English, Russian, Ukrainian, and now also a middling amount of German. I've studied Japanese and Latin in school, and I've made several constructed languages and scripts.

I have always been making visual art, and I have always been moving. Currently, I make mostly digital abstract art about the body.

My background in socio-technical research informs my artistic practice. For example, here is a recent work, based on a 2019 exhibition & performance with an exploratory simulation with Berlin-based artist Sage Jenson.

My code/data work experiences inform my artistic practice, too. For example, I have used (and sometimes made) tools for self-awareness and self-reflection since ~2007. Print/analog examples: Should I do the Thing? and Love-Song to a Future Self. I do not subscribe to lifehacking or the quantified self ethos; rather, I am interested in non-violent approaches that thrive with qualitative/imperfect data.
