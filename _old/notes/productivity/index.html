Meaning-o-Meter

Before we talk about productivity, we have to talk about its object: what actually are the things getting done? Here's one way to think about whether you should do the thing:

Is the thing worth doing by anyone? Yes No ...Kinda?

Is the thing worth doing by you in particular? Yes, I am well-positioned to make impact in this area. No, someone else would do it a lot better.

Those are harsh words! Are you sure that's not just impostor syndrome talking?

Lifehacking / Anti-lifehacking

2013, Seattle. Included in the Qualified Self essay

Lifehacking, as a word, implies a kind of internalized brutality; take an axe to your wrongness, hack it away. Hack together a tangle of stick-and-carrot punishment-reward strategies in the resulting wound. Do this for your work, your friendships, your finances, your closet, your soul, until you’ve hacked off all the wrong bits and replaced them all with fragile tips-and-tricks and a fountain of guilt gushing out of all the inevitable seams. The guilt is acid, and it erodes your sense of competence and wrecks the teetering tower of apps, and now you have a million reminders for a million tasks in a million todo apps, all of which you don’t use because the prospect of facing everything that you haven’t done is paralyzing. So you spend another hour hating yourself for staring at the soul-sucking blue of facebook.

I'm trying to do that less, but sometimes I don’t succeed.

I'm trying to be okay with that, too.
