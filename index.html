I do research, about tech+society, using an intriguing mix of qualitative Interviews, surveys, ethnographic observation; user research and quantitative Natural language processing, machine learning, stats as appropriate methods. See my CV/Resume (PDF) for my background. Currently, I am a doctoral candidate in Computer Science at the University of Washington, working with James Fogarty and Cecilia Aragon. My dissertation is about scientists, feelings, and software:

When the primary goal is to do good science, it is difficult to how to best invest time and energy needed to learn ever-changing computational tools and skills. What are most effective strategies that scientific groups use to create good software? Based on extensive interviews and observations of ~100 scientists, with a focus on ocean scientists.

Stay tuned! Expected PhD finish date: Spring '16

About me

My fiance and I smithing our rings at Chaos Communication Camp. We both originally grew up in different parts of Ukraine and emigrated in our teens: I to the US, he to Germany. We met in Istanbul in 2014, and then - on a wild whim - spent a month together in Morocco. It's been an adventure since. (Berlin-ish '15)

My favorite things include, but are not limited to: reading slowly and carefully, and/or aloud to people; bicycling at night, especially with a clear sky and a fat moon; poached eggs; poetry.

I don't have a favorite programming language, but currently I'm using python and C++, in iPython Notebook and the Arduino IDE environments respectively, a lot. Something about relational databases has always made me smile, but they're not part of my life right now.

I also do not have a favorite people-language, but I speak English, Russian, and Ukrainian with a native proficiency. I am actively learning German, because ich muss nach Berlin gehen! pretty soon. With less urgency, I'm brushing up on my Japanese, at which I used to proficient half a decade ago. And I've recently started learning basic reading/writing in Arabic.
